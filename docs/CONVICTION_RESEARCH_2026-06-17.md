## 1. The macro thesis is real (current data)

**Hyperscaler capex 2026:** ~$690 billion combined, +81% YoY, +207% vs 2024. Roughly 75% allocated to AI infrastructure.

- Amazon: $200B
- Alphabet: $175–185B
- Meta: $115–135B
- Microsoft: $120B+
- Oracle: $50B

**Power is the binding constraint, not money.** By 2028, data center power demand reaches ~74 GW with a projected ~49 GW shortfall in available supply. Every major hyperscaler reported by early 2026 that new capacity is gated by:

- Grid interconnect timelines: 18–36 months
- Transformer lead times: 18–24 months
- Permitting cycles: variable but lengthening

**This is the bottleneck your tickers sit on.** TE/IREN/HIVE/CLSK all own POWER (allocated grid capacity, often hydro or stranded) and CAPACITY (already-permitted sites, often originally for BTC mining). When hyperscalers need a place to put GPUs, they need power + site. The original BTC miners + emerging energy plays have inadvertently become the upstream supply for the AI infrastructure buildout.

**Rare earth angle:** China produces ~70% of global supply, controls ~90% of refining capacity. ~$60B of Western investment needed over the next decade. Project Vault and similar US programs are deploying. Bringing new rare earth mines online takes 15+ years. This is a longer-cycle thesis than the GPU buildout itself but it's the same shape: physical infrastructure with multi-year lead times now being asked to scale faster.

## 3. Bear cases I will respect (and you should too)

**Concentration risk at the broad market is at dot-com peak.** Magnificent 7 = 35% of S&P 500, identical to peak dot-com top-7 concentration. ~80% of 2025 market gains came from AI-related names. If the macro corrects, all four of your positions will correct *more* because they're higher-beta plays on the same theme.

**Capex/revenue mismatch is real.** Big Tech is spending $690B on AI capex against ~$12B/year in consumer AI revenue. MIT August 2025 study: 95% of organizations getting zero return on GenAI investments. The dot-com peak capex/sales ratio was 32%; the 2026 AI ratio is 34%, projected to hit 37% by 2028.

**Important nuance from the dot-com analogy:** the bust didn't stop the infrastructure being built from becoming durable — fiber optics and data centers built around 2000 powered Web 2.0. Same likely applies here: even if AI equity valuations correct hard, the *physical infrastructure* TE/IREN/HIVE/CLSK is putting in the ground will be valuable.

**Power grid risk is bidirectional.** It's the thesis (scarce resource = your tickers sit on it) AND a risk (permitting delays push schedules, hyperscalers pivot to whoever delivers fastest).

**Bank of England warned (early 2026) of AI valuation correction risk** specifically tied to infrastructure costs proving too high to support model economics. Worth knowing the central bank is publicly flagging this.

**What this all means for you:** the *companies* are real but the *market multiple* is at risk. Your edge isn't picking the boom (everyone sees it) — it's surviving a 30–50% drawdown in the middle of it without panic-selling, then accumulating more on weakness.

## 5. Position discipline rules (binding)

Pinned here so future-you and future-claude/codex can't talk you into bad behavior in real time:

1. **No single position above 40% of the long-term sleeve.** If IREN runs hard and crosses 40%, trim back to 35%. This rule survives even when "this time is different."

2. **Trim 25% if any position rallies 100%+ in 30 days.** Doesn't mean exit — just locks in some profit. The conviction stays; the size discipline doesn't.

3. **Add only on weakness OR on real catalyst.** "Weakness" means -10% from the 20-day high. "Real catalyst" means new signed contract, raised guidance, regulatory approval. Headline reaction without one of those = no add.

4. **Stop-add at -30% drawdown from your average entry.** If a position falls 30% below your cost basis, *stop adding* until you've re-read your thesis. The thesis might still be right; the position size is what gets you wiped out, not the thesis being wrong.

5. **Maximum 35% of total NLV in any single ticker, including legacy book.** Across both the long-term sleeve and any other holdings, no one company is more than 35% of net worth. This is the diversification floor.

6. **Re-research every position quarterly.** Once a quarter (calendar quarter end), re-read this doc against current data and update. Conviction without re-validation is hope; conviction with re-validation is discipline.
