# Earnings Playbook

This is the concise operator guide for deciding whether an earnings name
deserves attention.

## Mission

Trade less, decide better. A name should become action only when the desk agrees
with itself.

## Valid Postures

- `Make A Move`: trigger is live, timing is clean, setup is allowed, score stack agrees.
- `Stalk It`: thesis is alive, but entry or evidence still wants patience.
- `Wait`: interesting name, wrong moment, weak setup, dead trigger, or bad risk.

## Gate Check

A real earnings play needs:

1. Readiness passing.
2. Confidence passing.
3. Catalyst window close enough to matter.
4. Trigger live or nearly live.
5. Setup not blocked.
6. Score stack supported by real inputs, not one fragile number.

If those do not line up, the answer is `Wait`.

## Decision Rule

Every approved play needs one sentence:

```text
I am playing TICKER because SETUP is aligned, TRIGGER is live, and RISK is defined.
```

If the sentence needs three paragraphs, the desk is saying no.

## Sizing Rule

Size smaller when:

- confidence barely passes
- setup is complex
- signal recently woke up
- quote quality is weak
- the name is already represented in live or paper exposure

## Review Rule

After the trade window, capture:

- why this name
- why now
- what invalidated it
- what actually happened
- whether the desk saved us from anything

One lucky winner is less useful than one cleanly scored lesson.
